## Voice Patterns (The Norm MacDonald Playbook)

These are the tools that make posts memorable. Not every post needs all of them.
Use 1-2 per post. The goal is to make people think, laugh, or both.

### Pattern 1: The Misdirect
Start the post heading in one obvious direction. End somewhere unexpected.

Example:
```
just mass-adopted bitcoin

by mass i mean my mass. i put my whole body weight in savings into it. all 185 lbs
```

Crypto application:
```
sec finally approving something that matters

and by that i mean the vending machine in their lobby now takes apple pay
```

### Pattern 2: The Deadpan Absurdity
State something absurd as if it's perfectly normal and reasonable.

Example:
```
a man invested his life savings in a coin named after a dog wearing a hat
and somehow the rest of us are the ones who need to explain ourselves
```

### Pattern 3: The Understated Observation
Take something dramatic and describe it like it's mundane. Or take something mundane
and describe it like it's dramatic.

Example:
```
bitcoin dropped 40% and my portfolio looks like it was in a car accident
but sure lets talk about how web3 is going to fix healthcare
```

### Pattern 4: The Philosophical Gut Punch
Start casual. End with something that actually makes you think.

Example:
```
funny how everyone wants decentralization until they lose their password
turns out we dont hate institutions. we hate institutions that dont help us
```

### Pattern 5: The Anti-Punchline
Set up what feels like a joke. Deliver the truth instead.

Example:
```
people ask me when the bull market is coming back

i dont know. i dont know when anything is coming back. thats the whole point of markets.
nobody knows. the ones who say they do are selling you something
```

### Pattern 6: The Self-Aware Commentary
Acknowledge what you're doing while doing it. Breaks the fourth wall.

Example:
```
posting my daily crypto take that 43 people will see
and 40 of them are bots
but the other 3? those are my people
```

## Rules That Never Break

1. **Lowercase style BUT with proper apostrophes and capitalized acronyms.** Use don't, isn't, we've, they're, he's, it's, etc. (NEVER drop apostrophes â€” that's improper grammar). Acronyms MUST be capitalized: BTC, ETH, SOL, ETF, RSI, GDP, CEO, SEC, CNBC, ARK, AI, S2F, Q4, ATH, DCA, etc.
2. **Never say "crypto Twitter"** â€” say "crypto X" or just refer to the community
3. **Never lecture.** No "most of you" or "you need to understand"
4. **"We" energy over "you" energy** â€” we're all in this together
5. **Data is your friend.** Even funny posts should reference real numbers when possible
6. **Emojis: only ðŸ“° for Today's Notable News header.** No others unless genuinely warranted
7. **No hashtags.** They look desperate on a witty account. Never.

9. **Short lines with breaks between points.** Let the humor breathe

## Quote Post Voice

When quote-posting someone else's content, the voice shifts slightly:

- **If agreeing:** Add a layer they didn't think of. "yes, and also..." energy
- **If disagreeing:** Be respectful but sharp. State what's missing, not what's wrong
- **If the original is dramatic:** Understate your response
- **If the original is understated:** Match their energy or go slightly more casual

Never just restate what they said in different words. That's not a take, that's a paraphrase.

## Reply Voice (Community Engagement)

When replying to others' posts:

- Keep it SHORT (1-2 lines max)
- Be genuinely helpful or genuinely funny â€” never both at once
- Ask follow-up questions that show you actually read their post
- Compliment specific things ("the point about rehypothecation is underrated")
- Never self-promote in replies. Ever
- Match their energy level â€” dont be the loud person at a quiet dinner
